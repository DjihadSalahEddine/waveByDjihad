The car engine

background

The first combustion engine that has one-cylinder two-stroke unit was first designed a invented by the German Carl Benz in 1886.

"Benz"? I know that you are wondering where did you hear this word before. Yes, I know what you are thinking about, The most known car company "Mercedes-Benz". The brand's origins lie in Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft's 1901 Mercedes and Carl Benz's 1886 Benz Patent-Motorwagen, which is widely regarded as the first internal combustion engine in a self-propelled automobile.

A huge car engines evolution has accured since then. Now we have 60 brands. But only 14 are majors.

mechanism

The Car Engine may seem complicated but it won't be that complicated if you understand the main principle.

The air flows throught the inlet mainfold in the cylinder head. Fuls enters the engine chamber and it is also called the combustion chamber where it mixes with the air.
The air is directly sucked into the manifold from the throttle body. The fuel, on the other hand, is injected into the end of the manifold through a nozzle called the fuel injector.

The valve controles the entrance of air and fuel and the exist of exhaust gas. It also keeps the air-fuel mixture sealed inside the chamber.
The valves open and close based on which stroke is taking place. The opening and closing of the valves are done by an actuator rod known as the camshaft.

The engine pistol pressess the air-fuel mixture inside the chamber. the compression of the mixture gives more energy to the reaction. When it reaches the maximum pressure, the valves close and then the role of te spark plug comes in. It lunches a spark so the combustion reaction happen.

As a result, the pistol is pushed down translating the heat energy to the crankshaft then to the gears.

The exaust gases leave the chamber when the exhaust valve opens exiting the car due to the exhaist system.
